FAQ

Q: What materials do I need?

A: You can use slides, reading material, videos, interactive games, or anything else you find suitable!


Q: Where can I find the materials I need?

A: There are many free resources linked on the main page of this website, including short stories, dialogues, and videos, with beginner level, intermediate, and advanced difficulties. Of course, you are not limited to these options.


Q: What should I do if my student does not want to learn what I am teaching?

A: Change the topic! It's okay if you don't have a backup plan. You can ask your student what they would like to learn instead, or tell them to give you some time as you search for a new topic to teach. Be flexible!


Q: Do I need to be fluent in Chinese to teach well?

A: No! You can keep a translator open if you aren't fluent enough to explain phrases on your own. Just helping your student learn how to pronounce English words they may be struggling with is also a big help already.


Q: What should my time commitment be?

A: Outside of the 1.5 hours class time, you only need to prepare some teaching materials. This usually takes 15-30 minutes.


Q: What if technical issues arise?

A: The elderly students may have a hard time making sense of complicated controls or navigating websites. Please just be patient with them!
